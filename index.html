Counselling

You can use counselling to help you think more clearly, make decisions or changes in your life or come to terms with difficult experiences, both past and present. Counselling is a private time for you to talk about anything that is worrying you:

  • You won't be told what to do
  • You won't be judged or criticised
  • You will be listened to and supported in working things through
  • Counselling takes place in private - just you and your counsellor
  • Counsellors can offer one off counselling or a series of sessions. It's up to you how long you come
  • Appointments are weekly, and each session lasts for 50 minutes
  • There is no charge - our services are absolutely free - and confidential!

If you want counselling:

  • Before you start counselling, we will offer you an introductory appointment so you can find out how our service works
  • At the introductory appointment you can explore if counselling is for you or if another Off The Record service would be better
  • All counselling takes place at our Havant base
  • If you feel you don't get on with your counsellor, just ask to see someone else. We won't mind
  • There is no pressure. You choose whatever seems right for you.

Helping Off the Record

We would welcome any donation you could make so that we can continue providing our free services to children and young adults in need. We are heavily oversubscribed and unfortuantly we have a long waiting list. Any money raised will go to expanding and improving the services we offer and to driving down the waiting list times so our care and support can be given sooner. If you are unable to donate directly, you can still provide us valuable help by:

  • Organising or participating in an organised event on our behalf, for example:
    • Could you do a sponsored walk, swim, run or silence?
    • Could you bake and sell cakes to work colleagues?
    • Could you hold a coffee morning or dinner for friends?
  • Join our Friends of Off The Record scheme and "gift-aid" your donation.
  • Consider Off The Record in your annual charitable giving scheme.
  • Offering photocopying, printing or franking services
  • Donating stationery or refreshments.

Donating Monthly

Off the Record Volunteers have been supporting children and young people for almost 40 years. You can help us to continue to offer this valuable service by joining our list of supporters via the Friends of Off The Record. How to Fundraise to Off the Record South

One of the easiest ways to support the work we do is to organise a fund raising activity. This might seem like a lot of work but it doesn't need to be. Keep it simple and enlist the help of family members, friends, colleagues and your neighbourhood - everyone can make a difference and all you need is motivation and a little imagination.

Here are a few ideas:

  • Collection Boxes: Why not use one of our collection boxes at home for all your loose change, at the office for use as a swear box or place it in your local pub or convenience store . All you have to do is collect the tin from our Havant office and when it's full return it to us.
  • At work: Maybe we could become the nominated charity for the firm you work for or be the beneficiary of any sponsored events that your colleagues might be involved with.
  • At School: For example, do you have any contacts with schools or colleges that may want to know more about the work we do and help us to raise funds through non-uniform days?
  • At home: Why not organise a coffee morning, wine tasting, dinner with friends, a family BBQ or any other event that can bring friends and family together for fun and fund raising.
  • Web-page: Organise your fundraising on-line Make a fundraising page JustGiving

For more ideas click to download our fundraising toolkit.
